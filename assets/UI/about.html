Grand-Convergence was created by the de Koning Lab at the University of Calgary. Its development was led by Chenzhe Qian and Jason de Koning, with contributions from Nathan Bryans and Ivan Kryukov.

grand-conv was based on the original concept we implemented in codeML-Ancestral (de Koning, Castoe, Pollock, 2009), written by Jason while in David Pollock's lab. grand-conv is over 4,000 times faster than this earlier program (using the algorithm of Qian and de Koning, in prep.), and includes the Data Explorer (Qian*, Bryans*, Kryukov and de Koning, in prep.), which was written to help users better understand and interact with the output.
